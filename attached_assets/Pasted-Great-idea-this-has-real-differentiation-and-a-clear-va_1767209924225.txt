## 1. Product Vision (Business Framing)

**What the app does (in one sentence):**

> Users select a real-world item (e.g., bread, house, car, tuition) and instantly see how its price has changed over time when measured in **USD, Gold, and Bitcoin**, all on **one unified chart**.

**Why this is compelling:**

## 3. Key Technical Decisions (Important)

### A. Normalization (Critical Concept)

You **cannot** directly chart USD, gold ounces, and BTC raw prices together.

✅ Best approach:

* **Normalize all values to 100 at a starting year**
* Show *relative change*

Example:

* Bread in 1980 = 100
* Bread in 2024:

  * USD line = 350
  * Gold line = 110
  * Bitcoin line = 25

This makes the comparison instantly understandable.

---

## 4. Data Strategy (Very Important)

### A. What data you need

For each year:

1. **Item price in USD**
2. **Gold price (USD per ounce)**
3. **Bitcoin price (USD per BTC)**

Then compute:

* Item price in gold = USD price ÷ gold price
* Item price in BTC = USD price ÷ BTC price

---

### B. Data Sources (Recommended)

**Gold**

* LBMA historical prices
* World Gold Council
* FRED (Federal Reserve Economic Data)

**Bitcoin**

* CoinGecko API (free, reliable)
* CoinMarketCap (API key required)

**Item Prices**

* Government datasets (CPI components)
* FRED (excellent for:

  * Bread
  * Gas
  * Housing
  * Tuition)

📌 **Business Tip:**
Start with **3–5 items only**. Quality > quantity.
