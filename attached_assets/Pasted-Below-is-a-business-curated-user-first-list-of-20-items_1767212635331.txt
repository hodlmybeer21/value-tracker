Below is a business-curated, user-first list of 20 items that make the app genuinely useful for understanding real-world cost of living and purchasing power over time.

These are chosen based on:

Emotional relevance (“I buy this”)

Data availability going far back

Coverage of daily life + major life milestones

Clear storytelling when compared against Gold & Bitcoin

✅ Core Everyday Items (High Relatability)

Bread 🍞
Baseline food inflation anchor

Milk (1 gallon) 🥛
Staple household purchase

Eggs (dozen) 🥚
Volatile, memorable price changes

Gasoline (per gallon) ⛽
One of the most emotionally felt costs

Electricity (per kWh) ⚡
Invisible but unavoidable cost

Grocery Basket (CPI food index) 🛒
Overall food purchasing power

🏠 Housing & Living (Life-Defining Costs)

Median Home Price 🏠
Largest lifetime purchase for most people

Apartment Rent (average) 🏢
Relevant for younger users

Property Taxes (average) 🧾
Often overlooked but unavoidable

Home Insurance 🛡️
Quietly rising cost most don’t track

🎓 Education & Upward Mobility

College Tuition (public, in-state) 🎓
One of the most shocking charts

Student Loan Interest (avg rate) 📚
Cost of borrowing education

🚗 Transportation & Work

New Car Price (average) 🚗
Shows depreciation vs inflation

Used Car Price (average) 🚙
More relatable for most households

Public Transit Fare 🚆
Urban cost of living indicator

🏥 Health & Family

Health Insurance Premium (annual) 🏥
Major stress point for families

Doctor Visit (average cost) 🩺
Simple, powerful health inflation metric

📱 Modern Life Necessities

Mobile Phone Plan (monthly) 📱
Modern essential, good contrast vs deflation

Internet Service (monthly) 🌐
Required for work, school, life

💼 Work & Time (High Insight)

Average Hourly Wage ⏱️
Allows users to see if wages kept up

This item is especially powerful when inverted:
“How many hours of work does this cost?”

Why This List Is Strong (Strategically)

Covers daily survival, life milestones, and modern necessities

Creates “aha” moments when compared to gold & bitcoin

Keeps political bias low

Enables future features like:

“Hours of work required”

“Gold-denominated cost”

“Bitcoin-denominated cost”

Recommended Build Order (Smart Execution)

Bread

Gasoline

Median Home Price

College Tuition

Grocery Basket

Average Hourly Wage

These six alone tell a complete story.
