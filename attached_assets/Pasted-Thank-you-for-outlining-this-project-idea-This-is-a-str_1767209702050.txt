This is a strong, educational web app concept with clear value: helping users visualize purchasing power erosion (or preservation) in USD versus alternative stores of value like Gold and Bitcoin. By focusing on real-world items and presenting comparisons in **one clean, intuitive chart**, the app can highlight inflation dynamics and asset performance in an accessible way—perfect for financial education, investing audiences, or even viral sharing.

### Understood Requirements & Proposed Solution
- Users select a predefined item (e.g., median US home, gallon of gasoline, Big Mac).
- The app displays a single interactive line chart showing the historical cost of the item in:
  - USD (nominal price)
  - Gold (ounces needed to buy the item)
  - Bitcoin (BTC needed to buy the item)
- Chart format: Indexed to 100 at the earliest common date (typically ~2011–2014 when reliable BTC data begins). This normalizes the three lines for easy comparison on a linear scale—no log scale needed initially.
  - Rising line → item became more expensive in that unit (weaker purchasing power preservation).
  - Flat/falling line → stronger purchasing power preservation.
- Time range: Automatically limited to overlapping data availability (Gold has longest history, BTC shortest).

This approach mirrors successful sites like pricedingold.com, bitbo.io charts, and inflationchart.com, but combines all three units (USD + Gold + BTC) in one view with multiple selectable items—making our app more comprehensive and user-friendly.

### Proposed Initial Items (MVP Focus)
We'll start with 2–3 well-documented items to launch quickly:
1. **Median US Home Price** – Quarterly data from FRED/U.S. Census (1963–present). Iconic for showing long-term inflation/housing trends.
2. **Average US Gasoline Price** – Monthly data from EIA. Relatable everyday item.

### Technical Implementation on Replit
- **Language/Framework**: Python + Streamlit → Ideal for Replit. Fast prototyping, built-in interactivity (dropdowns, charts), deploys instantly as a public web app.
- **Key Libraries**:
  - streamlit
  - pandas (data handling)
  - plotly (interactive charts)
  - yfinance (real-time/historical Gold and Bitcoin prices)
- **Data Strategy**:
  - Gold & Bitcoin: Dynamically fetched via yfinance ('GC=F' for gold futures, 'BTC-USD' for Bitcoin)—daily data, reliable back to ~2000 for gold and 2014 for BTC.
  - Item prices: Static datasets embedded in the app (CSV uploaded to Replit or hardcoded dicts). Sourced from public/reliable places (FRED, EIA, Economist). Easy to update.
  - Processing: Resample/interpolate to common dates (monthly or quarterly), compute indexed costs.
- **Deployment**: One-file Streamlit app (main.py). Replit handles hosting—no server management needed.
